To help you learn more I have included many additional resources to many of the lecture videos. I highly recommend you read/watch them.

For the assignments, you may need to do some additional self-study depending on your skill level coming into this course. And as a programmer, you will need to search for answers your entire career - not just as a beginner. For that I have compiled some valuable sources of information:

C++ Complete Guide (Article where I explain many fundamental Unreal C++ concepts)

My Unreal Resource Link Collection (Tutorials, Samples, Tech videos, etc.)

AnswerHub & Forums (Mostly Community driven support)

Discord Community (Large Unreal Slackers with helpful community members)

Unreal Online Learning Portal (Free Video Courses on a variety of topics - mostly non-C++)

YouTube - In general I recommend being careful with tutorials taking too many shortcuts and fostering bad habits, but look for the absolute gems that are available too!

Alex Forsythe (some of the highest quality C++ youtube content I’ve found) Mathew Wadstein (bite-size tutorials) Unreal Engine playlists (I especially appreciate the Unreal Fest talks) Official Documentation (While sparse on C++, it does have an enormous amount of interesting information)

BeginPlay (Official Documentation) Learning paths for a variety of topics including engine structure, programming, Niagara, etc.

Community Wiki (after the original Wiki was axed, this new community effort has been working on another wiki)

And finally, My Twitter - while Twitter is not a great place to search for information, there are a ton of active developers sharing Unreal Engine tricks and knowledge daily.
